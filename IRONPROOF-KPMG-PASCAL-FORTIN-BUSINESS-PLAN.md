## THE MARKET — STRUCTURAL FORCES

### Canadian Sovereign AI Regulatory Pressure

Canada's regulatory landscape has created a mandatory, multi-layered proof-and-audit infrastructure with hard deadlines. The relevant instruments are:

**Bill C-27 / Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA).** AIDA died in Parliament in January 2025 with the dissolution of Parliament and the election call. It has not been reintroduced as of June 2026. However, this does not mean the regulatory pressure disappeared. AIDA's death left a vacuum that has been filled by sector-specific guidance from CCCS, Treasury Board, and OSFI — often more demanding than AIDA would have been, because they are operationally specific rather than principle-based. The federal government has signaled that AI governance legislation will return in a future session. The procurement requirements that AIDA would have mandated are being imposed now through contracting vehicles and departmental policies.

**CCCS Post-Quantum Cryptography Guidance.** CCCS issued its PQC transition guidance in 2022 and has aligned with NIST FIPS 203/204/205 (finalized August 2024). Federal departments and critical infrastructure operators are expected to complete inventory of cryptographic assets and begin migration planning. CNSA 2.0 (NSA's aligned guidance) mandates PQC for software signing by 2025, browsers/servers by 2027, and firmware by 2030. Every Canadian federal department with a cryptographic footprint is in scope.

**ITSG-33 SA&A (Security Assessment and Authorization).** The IT Security Risk Management framework mandates that federal IT systems undergo security assessment and authorization before deployment and on a recurring basis. KPMG is one of the primary delivery vehicles for ITSG-33 SA&A assessments across the federal government. These assessments currently produce narrative reports. The emerging expectation — driven by both Treasury Board and departmental security requirements — is that evidence artifacts must be independently verifiable.

**DND AI and Autonomous Systems Policy.** DND's 2023 Responsible Artificial Intelligence strategy and the IDEaS (Innovation for Defence Excellence and Security) program have created a formal requirement for AI systems in defence contexts to produce verifiable behavioral evidence. DND awarded $1.5M under IDEaS CFP-6 CH13 for sovereign AI pipeline development. The requirement for machine-verifiable AI behavioral constraints is explicit in DND procurement criteria.

**OSFI Guideline B-13 (Technology and Cyber Risk Management).** OSFI's B-13, effective January 2024, requires federally regulated financial institutions to maintain formal evidence of technology risk controls. AI systems in financial institutions now require audit trails that regulators can independently verify. OSFI B-13 is the financial sector equivalent of the federal AI governance requirement.
